## 📌 Overview
This project showcases a comprehensive sales data analysis performed using Microsoft Excel. The analysis focuses on transforming raw sales data into actionable insights through data cleaning, KPI analysis, pivot tables, interactive dashboards, and data visualization techniques.

This project demonstrates practical data analysis and business intelligence skills using Excel tools commonly used in real-world business environments.

## 🎯 Project Objectives
•	Analyze overall sales performance 
•	Identify top-performing product lines 
•	Evaluate branch and city performance 
•	Understand customer purchasing behavior 
•	Track important business KPIs 
•	Analyze payment methods and transaction trends 
•	Build an interactive dashboard for data exploration 
•	Present insights visually for business decision-making 

📂 Dataset Information
The dataset contains transactional retail sales data collected from multiple branches across four different years.
Each year in the dataset represents sales transactions recorded for a single day rather than a continuous yearly dataset.

## 🔍 Analysis Performed
Due to the dataset structure, the analysis focuses on comparative business insights and trend observation rather than full long-term forecasting.

### Dashboard Development
Built interactive dashboards with slicers to:
•	Dynamically compare yearly performance 
•	Filter data by branch, product line, and year 
•	Track KPIs and business metrics visually 
•	Monitor returns and sales trends efficiently

### Sales & Returns Analysis 
•	Calculated returned orders percentage 
•	Analyzed quantity sold versus returned quantity per product line 
•	Identified branches with the highest return rates 
•	Compared sales vs. returns performance across branches

### Representative (REP) Performance Analysis
•	Evaluated sales representative performance 
•	Compared average representative ratings 

### Year-over-Year (YoY) Analysis
Compared the selected year in slicers with the previous year to identify business trends in:
•	Product line sales 
•	Product line returns 
•	Branch sales performance 
•	Branch return performance
• Average customer rating

### Payment Method Analysis
•	Analyzed customer payment preferences 

### Time Trend Analysis
•	Explored sales trends over time 
•	Identified peak business periods and seasonal patterns 
 
## 📌 Final Conclusion
The analysis revealed several important business insights across sales, profitability, and returns performance over multiple years.
•	Despite the continuous growth in total sales over the four-year period, the relationship between sales, profit, and costs remained highly consistent. Profit represented approximately 25% of total sales, while COGS consistently accounted for nearly 75%, indicating stable operational margins over time. 
•	Sales performance showed an overall positive upward trend compared to previous years. However, return rates also increased alongside sales growth, highlighting the importance of improving product quality control and return management strategies. 
•	The Food and Beverages product line requires further business attention, as it recorded the lowest sales performance while simultaneously generating the highest number of returns in the latest year. This may indicate issues related to product quality, customer satisfaction, or inventory management. 
•	Customer purchasing behavior appeared relatively balanced between customer types, with:
o	Members contributing approximately 54% of total sales 
o	Normal customers contributing around 46% 
This suggests that both customer groups play a significant role in overall revenue generation, with no extreme dependency on a single customer segment.
•	Branch and product line comparisons revealed variations in return behavior and sales efficiency, emphasizing opportunities for operational optimization and targeted business improvements. 
Overall, the project demonstrates how Excel-based business analysis can be used to transform raw transactional data into actionable insights that support strategic decision-making.
